Improving Checkout Flow

Reduced checkout abandonment by 42% and increased conversion by 28%

E-commerce Platform ‚Ä¢ 2023 ‚Ä¢ Product Manager

The Problem

Our checkout process had a 68% abandonment rate, with users dropping off primarily at the payment information step. User research revealed that the form was too long, lacked clear progress indicators, and didn't save information between sessions.

Our Solution

We redesigned the checkout flow with a multi-step approach, implemented auto-save functionality, and added progress indicators. We also integrated one-click payment options and simplified the form fields to only essential information.

Key Features:

  • ‚Ä¢ Multi-step checkout with progress indicator
  • ‚Ä¢ Auto-save functionality for returning users
  • ‚Ä¢ One-click payment options (Apple Pay, Google Pay)
  • ‚Ä¢ Simplified form fields (only essential information)
  • ‚Ä¢ Mobile-optimized design

My Process

1

Research Phase

Conducted user interviews and analyzed analytics data to identify pain points. Found that 68% of users abandoned at payment step.

2

Design Phase

Created wireframes and prototypes for the new multi-step flow. Tested with 50 users in usability sessions.

3

Testing Phase

Ran A/B tests with 10,000 users to validate the new design. New flow showed 42% reduction in abandonment.

4

Implementation

Worked with engineering to build and launch the new checkout experience. Monitored metrics and iterated based on user feedback.

The new checkout flow resulted in a 42% reduction in abandonment rate and increased conversion by 28%. Users completed checkout 2.3x faster on average, leading to $2.1M in additional revenue in the first quarter after launch.
